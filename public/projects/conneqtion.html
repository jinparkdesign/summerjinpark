OVERVIEW

With my team members, I dicided to develop a communication app for Seneca students. The app is called “ConneQtion” (Connection + Question). It is an interactive communication app designed for Seneca students, which allows users to post their questions and concerns with regards to courses and assignments - much like a student version of Quora. The purpose of the app is to assist first-term students by providing them with a user-friendly platform, where their questions may be answered quickly.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

First-term students are overwhelmed with the amount of school works, and some of them lacks confidence to ask questions face to face becuase they are introvert. When they have assignments to finish, most students in class have the same issue, but it is hard to communicate with each other and find the right answer. Communication via email is sometimes uncomfortable for them because it requires formality and it is slow.

EMPATHY MAP

We first created an empathy map. Through this, we were able to empathize with potential users and gain a deeper insight into them. We crafted a persona who goes by the name, Geo, and established what his current pain points, goals and tasks would be as a first-year student.

The first part is the user’s task. How does a user complete his task and communicate his questions in order to get a valid response? In Geo’s case, he finishes his assignments on time and understands what is required for each assignment. Ideally, he would like to be aware of what other students’ issues or questions are regarding assignments in order to improve his understanding of what is required of him.

The second part is the user’s Influences. What people, things may influence how the user acts? In this case for Geo, he is content to mostly keep to himself as he relies on the support of his close circle of friends and family.

The third part is the user’s feeling. How is the user feeling about the experience? What really matters to them? Geo lacks confidence to ask questions in front of the class because he is an introvert. He is also overwhelmed with the amount of school work, so he is often stressed. He hopes that there would be an open discussion forum where he can address his issues.

The fourth part is the user’s pain points. What pain points might the user be experiencing that they hope to overcome? As an introvert, Geo is uncomfortable when it comes to face-to-face conversation with someone so he frequently relies on digital communication such as messaging or social media. Also, English is not his first language, so he often gets mixed up with words he has not heard of and finds it difficult to make sense of more complex sentences. Not being able to properly communicate is his greatest pain point. He hopes he can overcome it.

And lastly, the user’s overall goal. What is the user’s ultimate goal? What are they trying to achieve? Since Geo has trouble communicating, he would like to find a way to QUICKLY address his questions and concerns on a digital platform, which is open to ALL students and instructors in his program.

JOURNEY MAP SCENARIO

Geo is an international student enrolled in the Seneca INM program. He has sent his professor a couple of emails regarding his upcoming essay assignment, but has not yet received a response. He is unsure of how to format the essay. The essay format information was included in the presentation slides, which were given during class. However, Geo did not attend said class, and the presentation slides have not yet been posted on Blackboard. He would like to figure out how to format his essay as soon as possible

SOLUTION

Creating an open discussion forum where Seneca students can address their issues and concerns. In the forum, students can post their question and wait for someone to respond and also can be aware of what other students’ issues or questions are regarding assignments in order to improve his understanding of what is required. The app includes feature which allows user to bookmark someone else's question and receive notificaions of answers for saved questions. They can categorize their questions in a specific course. There are several 'sory by' options for viewing by courses, recent post, popular and announcement ect.

FINAL MOCK-UPS

Welcome& Login Page

Welcome page is the first screen that users see when they open this app. We put an illustration and short introduction about the app. When you click the orange button at the bottom, the next page, login page, will appear.

Home Feed Page & Post and Comment

In the home feed page, you can see questions and announcement posts uploaded by students and professors. Students can share their concern about any assignment and get a quick answer from anyone. If you want to leave a comment, you can simply click the comment button in the middle. You can see the posts and comments from the other students. There is an image and attachment features for users to share images and their works through the comment bar.

Post Sorted By...

You are able to see the feed page sorted by selected options. You can select multiple options. In this case, the options of ‘INM100’ & ‘Recent’ are selected, and recent & INM100 posts are now first on the top

Post Question Page

There is a bar on the top where students and instructors can post questions and announcements. If you click it, you are able to choose which course it is for and an option either question or announcement depending on the post. So when other people see your post, it is easy to see what the post is about.

Recent Search Page

Search bar is for searching posts by keywords. When you click it, you can see your recent search, or you can simply click the specific course icon. You can always delete your recent search for privacy reasons.

Notification Page & Setting Page

Whenever professors and students upload a post or leave a comment, you receive a notification. you can customize the notification from the setting page. In setting page, you can edit your account, block posts/people, set privacy/general.
