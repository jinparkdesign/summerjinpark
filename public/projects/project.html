OVERVIEW

eCycle is a mobile app that helps user to rent electrical bikes conveniently with ease. It provides tutorials on how to use the app and the docking stations, traffic laws, safety rules and choosing the length of time for renting step by step. It also displays the e-bike stations based on user’s current location on the map. Users can also view how many e-bikes and docks are available through this app. Renting through the app is faster than the kiosk because the user can save their payment info and pay through the app in advance.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Users often walk to the e-bike rent station and find out that there is no available e-bikes to rent. So, every time it happens, a user needs to go to another nearby station with no assurance. They are not sure about the exact locations of the stations especially if they are not local. They end up searching for the location through Google map or e-bike’s official website. Also, they want to check the number of available bikes and docks without actually walking there. They also feel that the process of renting an e-bike from a kiosk was complicated and time consuming. Sometimes there are even long lines at the bike kiosk. It would be helpful if there was a simpler way to rent a bike in advance.

SOLUTION

The app can guide the user on how to rent e-bikes with an intuitive on-boarding process. Once the user is familiar in the process of renting a bike, the app can provide useful information such as location of the rental stations and the availability of the bikes. It will use the user’s current location to display the closest station or they can search an area for the stations. It also simplifies the process of users renting an e-bike. Rather than going to a kiosk every time and entering your information, credit card and duration, you can save time by using the app that saves the user’s profile. When you rent a bike with the app, you will receive a passcode to unlock the bike which can be used within an hour. Renting a bicycle becomes easy to do at home or on the way ahead of time with a mobile app.

PRODUCT FINAL MOCK-UPS

Splash and Sign-up Page

After the splash screen, the user can either sign up or log in these screens. Also, they can log in with Apple, Facebook or Google accounts.

Onboarding

Onboarding screen shows how the app works and safety rules and regulations about e-cycle. The cards can be swiped left and right to navigate. The user has option to skip as well.

Main Screen

The user lands on the main screen which shows a map that displays the locations of the e-cycle stations based on the user's current location. They can search a place or station in the search bar. The left menu shows some features like feature, ride history, help and account. The right menu shows e-cycles in a list view. There is an option to sort by either distance or battery power.

Station Information and e-cycle Detail

Once the user selects a station, it displays available e-cycles, distance from the user and empty docks. Then they can select an available e-cycle and see the details such as location, power, max distance and max speed. There is also an option to mark a station as favourite.

Make a Reservation

The user can choose a duration and make a reservation. They have the option to pay with their credit card, Apple/Samsung pay and Paypal. The payment information can be saved for future reservations. Then, the user has to agree to the terms and conditions to complete the reservation.

Cancellation

After an e-cycle is reserved, the app navigates the user to the station. They can also cancel their reservation within one hour.

Bike Unlock

When the user arrives at the station, they can unlock the bike with the code that is provided by the app. The app displays the battery life and how much time is left and they have the option to extend their ride.

Other Features

There are other features such as favorite, ride history, help, articles about benfits of e-cycle, account details, and report issue.

DESIGN PROCESS

MARKET RESEARCH - COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

I conducted a competitive analysis first. The companies that I researched were Bixy, Bond and Lime. I listed the important features and evaluated the strengths and weaknesses of each competition. This allowed me to identify industry trends and have a sense of how to give my company an advantage.

STORYBOARD

I created a storyboard to easily understand and remember the situation and problems at first glance. This is based on a user story. With this storyboard, I, as a designer can understand what's going on in the user's world and encourage to think about a solution to solve the user's daily challenges.

USER PERSONA

Creating user persona helps to understand user's experience, pain points, goals and needs. User persona is one of the best ways to step out of myself and empathize and identyfy with the user I am desining for.

USER FLOW DIAGRAM

This user flow diagram shows how the users will navigate through the app. This helps me think about what happens to the user before and after they visit a particular page and focus on what is important.
